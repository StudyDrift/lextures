# Values and money

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

Money decisions are easier when they connect to what you care about — security, family, learning, freedom, generosity, or something else. Values are not the same as a budget category; they are the **why** behind goals.

## Why mindset matters

People often feel shame or pressure about money. A useful starting point is curiosity: What does "enough" look like for you right now? What trade-offs are you willing to make?

Federal financial-education materials emphasize building skills step by step rather than chasing perfect answers. The [MyMoney Five](https://www.mymoney.gov/mymoney-five-tools) framework from [MyMoney.gov](https://www.mymoney.gov/) organizes core habits into five themes: **Earn**, **Save & Invest**, **Protect**, **Spend**, and **Borrow**.
