# 50/30/20 and zero-based budgeting

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

There is no single "correct" budget method. Two common starting points:

## 50/30/20 guideline

A popular **guideline** (not a law) suggests roughly:

| Share | Use |
|---|---|
| **~50%** | Needs (essentials) |
| **~30%** | Wants |
| **~20%** | Savings and debt repayment beyond minimums |

Your housing costs or local prices may make 50/30/20 unrealistic. Treat it as a **compass**, then adjust.

## Zero-based budgeting

In a **zero-based** plan, every dollar of planned income is assigned a job (bills, savings, debt, or spending) until planned income minus planned allocations equals zero. "Zero" means **fully assigned**, not "spend everything impulsively."

## Choosing

- Use **50/30/20** if you want a simple first draft.
- Use **zero-based** if you need tighter control or irregular income.
