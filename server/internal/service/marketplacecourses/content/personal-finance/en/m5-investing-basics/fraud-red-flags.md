# Investment-fraud red flags

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

[Investor.gov](https://www.investor.gov/) and the [CFPB](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/) warn about common fraud patterns. Classic **red flags** include:

- **Guaranteed high returns** with little or no risk
- Pressure to act **immediately** or keep the deal **secret**
- Unsolicited tips, especially via social media or cold contact
- Requests for unusual payment methods or remote access to devices/accounts
- Unregistered sellers or products you cannot verify

## Safer habits

- Verify registration and firm information using official tools linked from Investor.gov.
- Be skeptical of anyone who discourages questions or independent research.
- Remember: legitimate investments can lose money; "can't lose" claims are a warning sign.

Read more: [Investor.gov — avoiding fraud](https://www.investor.gov/) and CFPB fraud/consumer protection pages at [consumerfinance.gov](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/).
