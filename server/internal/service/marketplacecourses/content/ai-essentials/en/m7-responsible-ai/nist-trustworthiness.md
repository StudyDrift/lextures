# NIST trustworthiness characteristics

The [NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)](https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework) describes characteristics of trustworthy AI. Use them as a checklist when judging a use case:

1. **Valid and reliable** — does what it claims, consistently enough for the context
2. **Safe** — does not create unacceptable safety risks
3. **Secure and resilient** — resists attacks and recovers from failures
4. **Accountable and transparent** — people can understand roles, processes, and limitations
5. **Explainable and interpretable** — decisions can be understood at the needed level
6. **Privacy-enhanced** — protects personal data appropriately
7. **Fair — with harmful bias managed** — seeks equitable outcomes and mitigates harmful bias

You do not need to memorize legal text. You **do** need to map real scenarios to these ideas and name mitigations (human oversight, data minimization, evaluation, access control).
