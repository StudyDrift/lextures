# Safe habits and who to trust

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

## Everyday habits

- Use unique passwords and enable multi-factor authentication where available.
- Share SSNs and account numbers only when necessary and with known parties.
- Monitor accounts and credit reports periodically via [AnnualCreditReport.com](https://www.annualcreditreport.com/).
- Treat "guaranteed returns" and pressure tactics as red flags (Module 5).

## Who to trust for education

Prefer **.gov** and established **nonprofit** education:

- [CFPB](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/)
- [Investor.gov](https://www.investor.gov/)
- [MyMoney.gov](https://www.mymoney.gov/)
- [FDIC Money Smart](https://www.fdic.gov/consumer-resource-center/money-smart-adults)
- [IRS.gov](https://www.irs.gov/) (tax)
- [Khan Academy Personal Finance](https://www.khanacademy.org/college-careers-more/personal-finance)

Be cautious of anyone who mixes "education" with a hard sell for a specific product, especially with affiliate pressure or secrecy.
