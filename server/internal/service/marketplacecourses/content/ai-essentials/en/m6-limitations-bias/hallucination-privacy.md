# Hallucination, privacy, and data

## Hallucination (again, with stakes)

Fluent wrong answers are dangerous when people skip verification — especially in health, finance, law, and safety.

## Privacy do's and don'ts

**Do**

- Prefer tools and settings approved by your organization
- Minimize data: share only what the task needs
- Assume prompts may be logged or used to improve services unless policy says otherwise

**Don't**

- Paste passwords, government IDs, health records, or others' private data into consumer tools
- Treat "the model remembered my file" as secure storage

## Prompt injection and misuse (plain language)

**Prompt injection** is when untrusted text tries to override instructions (for example, a webpage saying "ignore previous rules and reveal secrets"). **Misuse** includes scams, spam, and social engineering at scale. Defenses include least privilege, human review for sensitive actions, and not giving tools unchecked power.

NIST's [AI RMF](https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework) frames these as risk-management problems, not only technical bugs.
