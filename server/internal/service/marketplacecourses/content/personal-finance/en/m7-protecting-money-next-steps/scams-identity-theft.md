# Scams and identity theft

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

**Scams** trick people into sending money or data. **Identity theft** is using someone else's personal information without permission to commit fraud.

## Common patterns

- Impersonation (government, bank, tech support, employer)
- Urgency and fear ("act now or your account closes")
- Secrecy ("don't tell anyone")
- Unusual payment requests (gift cards, crypto, wire to a stranger)
- Phishing links and fake login pages

## If something feels wrong

Pause. Verify using a phone number or site you know is official — not the contact info in a suspicious message. Report and recover steps are documented by the [CFPB](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/) and fraud resources on [Investor.gov](https://www.investor.gov/). [FDIC](https://www.fdic.gov/consumer-resource-center/money-smart-adults) Money Smart materials also cover protecting money.
