# Risk, return, and diversification

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

Investing means putting money to work with the expectation of future returns — and the possibility of **loss**. Higher expected returns usually come with higher risk. There is no guaranteed high return with little risk.

## Diversification

**Diversification** means spreading investments so one failure does not sink the whole plan. It reduces some risks; it does not eliminate market risk.

## Building blocks (definitions only — not recommendations)

| Term | Plain idea |
|---|---|
| **Stocks** | Ownership shares in companies; can be volatile |
| **Bonds** | Loans to issuers; generally different risk profile than stocks |
| **Funds** | Baskets of holdings (including **index funds** that track an index) |

This course does **not** recommend specific securities, "best" asset classes, or timing strategies.
