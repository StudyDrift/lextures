# APR and types of debt

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

**APR (Annual Percentage Rate)** expresses the yearly cost of borrowing, including interest and certain fees, so you can compare credit products more fairly.

## "Good" vs. "bad" debt (careful language)

Educators sometimes call debt that helps build long-term capacity (for example education or a reliable work vehicle, when affordable) differently from high-cost debt used for short-lived consumption. Labels are imperfect: any debt can become harmful if payments are unmanageable.

## Credit cards

Credit cards can be useful tools or expensive traps depending on how balances are carried. Interest on revolving balances can grow quickly. Read terms, know the APR, and understand minimum payments.

CFPB materials on credit cards, loans, and debt are available at [consumerfinance.gov](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/).
