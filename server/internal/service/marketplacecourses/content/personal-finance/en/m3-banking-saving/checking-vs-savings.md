# Checking vs. savings

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

| Account type | Typical use | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Checking** | Everyday spending, bill pay, debit | Often lower interest; watch fees and overdraft policies |
| **Savings** | Short- to medium-term reserves | Often higher interest than checking; may limit some transfers |

"High-yield" savings accounts generally offer higher **APY** than traditional savings, but rates change. Compare fees, access, and insurance status — not marketing slogans alone.

Consumer explainers from the [CFPB](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/) and [FDIC](https://www.fdic.gov/consumer-resource-center/money-smart-adults) cover choosing and using deposit accounts.
