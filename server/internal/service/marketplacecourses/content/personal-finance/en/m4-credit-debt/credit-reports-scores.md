# Credit reports and scores

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

A **credit report** is a record of your credit accounts and payment history. A **credit score** is a number lenders often use to estimate credit risk based on report information.

## Free annual credit reports

In the United States, you can get free credit reports through the federally authorized site [AnnualCreditReport.com](https://www.annualcreditreport.com/). Reviewing reports helps you spot errors or accounts you do not recognize.

## What typically influences scores

Common factors discussed in consumer education include payment history, amounts owed, length of credit history, new credit, and credit mix. Exact models vary — treat marketing claims carefully.

## Good habits (educational, not personalized advice)

- Pay at least the minimum on time when you use credit.
- Keep utilization manageable relative to limits when possible.
- Check reports periodically for accuracy.

Learn more from the [CFPB](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/) credit and debt guides and [FDIC Money Smart](https://www.fdic.gov/consumer-resource-center/money-smart-adults).
