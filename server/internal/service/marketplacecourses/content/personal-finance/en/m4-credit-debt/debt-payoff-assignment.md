# Debt-Payoff Plan

**Goal:** Choose avalanche or snowball and order sample debts (learning outcome 4).

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

**Privacy warning:** Use **illustrative or learner-chosen figures only**. Do **not** enter real account numbers, balances, Social Security numbers, passwords, or other sensitive personal data into this assignment or into third-party calculators.

## Sample debts (illustrative — use these or your own made-up set)

| Debt | Balance | APR | Minimum |
|---|---|---|---|
| Card A | $2,400 | 22% | $60 |
| Card B | $900 | 18% | $35 |
| Personal loan | $4,000 | 11% | $120 |

Assume you can put **$200 extra** per month toward debt after minimums.

## Submit

1. **Strategy:** avalanche or snowball.
2. **Order:** list the payoff order for the three debts under your strategy.
3. **Justification:** 3–5 sentences on why you chose that strategy (interest cost vs. motivation, etc.).
4. Confirm you used **illustrative** figures only.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Full credit |
|---|---|
| Strategy named | Avalanche or snowball |
| Order correct for that strategy | Avalanche = highest APR first; snowball = smallest balance first |
| Justification | Clear, good-faith reasoning |
| Privacy | No real account numbers or SSNs |
