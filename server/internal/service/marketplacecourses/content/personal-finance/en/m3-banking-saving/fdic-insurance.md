# FDIC deposit insurance

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

The **Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC)** insures deposits at **FDIC-insured banks** if the bank fails, up to the **standard insurance amount** per depositor, per insured bank, for each ownership category. Credit unions have a parallel system through the NCUA.

## What to remember

- Confirm the institution is insured before relying on coverage.
- Coverage has **limits** and rules by ownership category — check current details on official FDIC materials rather than memorizing a number from an old article.
- FDIC insurance covers **deposit accounts** (like checking and savings) — it is **not** a guarantee against investment losses in stocks, bonds, or crypto.

Learn more via [FDIC Money Smart for Adults](https://www.fdic.gov/consumer-resource-center/money-smart-adults) and FDIC consumer resources linked from that hub. [MyMoney.gov](https://www.mymoney.gov/) also points learners to trustworthy banking basics.
