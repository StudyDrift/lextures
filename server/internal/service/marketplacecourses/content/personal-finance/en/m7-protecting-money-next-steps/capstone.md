# Capstone: My Simple Financial Plan

**Goal:** Assemble a one-page plan demonstrating outcomes 1–7.

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

**Privacy warning:** Use **illustrative or learner-chosen figures only**. Do **not** enter real account numbers, balances, Social Security numbers, passwords, or other sensitive personal data into this assignment or into third-party calculators.

**Grader note:** Feedback (if any) comments on **completeness and clarity only**. It must **not** give personalized financial, tax, or investment advice.

## Submit all five parts

**(1) SMART goal** — one goal with specific amount/timeline (illustrative OK).

**(2) Budget snapshot** — monthly income and totals for needs / wants / savings-or-debt (illustrative table or bullets).

**(3) Emergency-fund target** — a starter or longer-term target and where you would keep it conceptually (e.g., "savings account" — no real account numbers).

**(4) Debt or savings step** — one next action (e.g., avalanche order on sample debts, or automate $X illustrative savings).

**(5) Fraud-protection habit** — one concrete habit (e.g., verify bank messages via official app/site; freeze/monitor credit; refuse gift-card payment requests).

## Length

About one page (roughly 250–500 words) or a compact structured outline covering all five parts.
