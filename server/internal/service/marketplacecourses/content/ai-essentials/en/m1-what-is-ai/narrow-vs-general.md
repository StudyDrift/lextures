# Narrow AI vs. general AI

Almost every AI system you use today is **narrow AI** (also called weak AI): it is good at a specific task or family of tasks, and brittle outside that scope.

## Narrow AI

Narrow systems do one job well — recommend songs, detect tumors in scans (when carefully validated), or complete sentences. They do not "understand the world" the way a person does. A chess engine that beats grandmasters cannot drive a car.

## Artificial general intelligence (AGI)

**AGI** would mean AI that can learn and perform a wide range of intellectual tasks at a human-like level across domains. AGI is a research goal and a public debate — **not** a product you can buy today. Marketing that implies current chatbots are "generally intelligent" overstates what they do.

## Why the distinction matters

When a tool fails outside its training distribution, that is expected for narrow AI. Judging fit means asking: *Is this task inside the system's designed scope?* If not, use a different tool or keep a human in the loop.

Andrew Ng's non-technical course [AI for Everyone](https://www.deeplearning.ai/courses/ai-for-everyone) emphasizes practical AI literacy without assuming AGI is already here.
