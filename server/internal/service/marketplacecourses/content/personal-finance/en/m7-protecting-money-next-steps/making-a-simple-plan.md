# Making a simple financial plan

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

You do not need a 40-page plan. A **one-page** plan that you revisit beats a perfect plan you never write.

## Minimum viable plan

1. One **SMART** goal
2. A rough **budget snapshot** (income vs. major categories)
3. An **emergency-fund** target (even a small starter amount)
4. One **debt or savings** next step
5. One **fraud-protection** habit

Use illustrative figures. Revisit quarterly. When life changes (new job, move, family), update the plan.

This page prepares you for the capstone. Official frameworks like [MyMoney Five](https://www.mymoney.gov/mymoney-five-tools) can help you check that Earn, Spend, Save & Invest, Borrow, and Protect are not ignored.
