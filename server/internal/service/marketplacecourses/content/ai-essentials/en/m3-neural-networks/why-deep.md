# Why "deep," and what made it work

**Deep** learning means many layers. Depth helps models build hierarchical representations — edges → shapes → objects in vision; sounds → phonemes → words in speech.

## What made modern deep learning practical

Three ingredients scaled together:

1. **Large datasets**
2. **More compute** (especially specialized hardware)
3. **Better training methods and architectures**

None of these alone is "intelligence." Together they enabled breakthroughs on perception and language tasks.

## What deep learning is good and bad at

**Often strong:** pattern recognition in high-dimensional data (images, audio, text) when plenty of relevant data exists.

**Often weak or risky:** tasks needing reliable causal reasoning, guaranteed truth, or fairness under skewed data — without careful design, evaluation, and human oversight.
