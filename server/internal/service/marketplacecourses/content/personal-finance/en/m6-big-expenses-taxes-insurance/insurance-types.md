# Insurance types and matching risk

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

**Insurance** transfers certain financial risks to an insurer in exchange for premiums. The educational goal is to **match coverage to risks you cannot easily afford**, not to sell a policy.

| Type | Risk it commonly addresses |
|---|---|
| Health | Medical costs |
| Auto | Vehicle accidents/liability (where required/needed) |
| Renters / homeowners | Belongings and related liability/property risks |
| Disability | Income loss from illness/injury (when available) |
| Life | Financial support for dependents after death (situation-dependent) |

## Matching risk

Ask: What loss would disrupt my finances? What coverage is required by law or a lender? What deductible can I afford?

Shop carefully, compare declarations pages, and use trusted consumer resources such as the [CFPB](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/) and [FDIC Money Smart](https://www.fdic.gov/consumer-resource-center/money-smart-adults) rather than high-pressure sales alone.
