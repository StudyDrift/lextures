# Housing, vehicles, and student aid

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

## Housing

Rent vs. buy decisions depend on local markets, credit, down payment, time horizon, and personal factors. Total housing cost includes more than rent/mortgage (utilities, insurance, maintenance, fees). Consumer guides on mortgages and renting appear on [CFPB](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/).

## Vehicles

Beyond the sticker price, budget for insurance, fuel/maintenance, registration, and depreciation. A reliable cheaper car can be the better financial fit than a status upgrade.

## Student aid (U.S.)

Federal student aid has official processes and forms. Start from trusted government information rather than paid "aid consultants" who cold-contact you. For tax interactions with education benefits, prefer [IRS.gov](https://www.irs.gov/) and official aid sites linked from reputable .gov hubs. [Khan Academy Personal Finance](https://www.khanacademy.org/college-careers-more/personal-finance) also covers education-cost topics in plain language.

Plan big expenses inside your budget: save ahead when you can, compare total cost of ownership, and avoid stretching payments until cash flow breaks.
