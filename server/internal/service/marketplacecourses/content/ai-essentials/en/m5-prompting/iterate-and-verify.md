# Iterate and verify outputs

Rarely is the first prompt perfect. **Iteration** means changing one thing at a time — add a constraint, ask for sources to check, request a shorter version — and comparing results.

## Verification checklist

Before you trust an output:

1. Does it answer the actual question?
2. Are facts checkable against a reliable source?
3. Did it invent citations, numbers, or quotes?
4. Would a domain expert accept this as a draft?

## Hallucination

**Hallucination** means confident-sounding content that is false or unsupported. Spotting it is a core literacy skill: look for specifics you can verify, and be extra skeptical of precise statistics and niche citations.

Treat any sample AI output in this course as **illustrative** — it may be wrong.
