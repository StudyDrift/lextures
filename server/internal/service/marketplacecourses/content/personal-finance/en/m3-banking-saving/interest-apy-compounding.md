# Interest, APY, and compound growth

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

**Interest** is the cost of borrowing or the return for depositing money. For savings, a higher rate generally means faster growth — all else equal.

## APY vs. interest rate

**Annual Percentage Yield (APY)** reflects the effective yearly return **including compounding**. When comparing deposit accounts, APY is often the fairer apples-to-apples number.

## Compound interest

**Compound interest** means you earn interest on your original amount **and** on interest already added. Over long periods, compounding can matter a lot — which is why starting earlier (even with smaller amounts) is a common teaching point.

Try the official [Investor.gov Compound Interest Calculator](https://www.investor.gov/financial-tools-calculators/calculators/compound-interest-calculator) with **illustrative** numbers (you will use it formally in Module 5).
