# Snowball vs. avalanche payoff

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

When you can pay more than minimums, two common strategies order the **extra** payments:

| Method | Extra payment goes to… | Often chosen because… |
|---|---|---|
| **Avalanche** | Highest **interest rate** first | Mathematically may reduce total interest |
| **Snowball** | Smallest **balance** first | Quicker "wins" may help motivation |

Keep making minimums on all debts. Choose a method you can stick with. Neither method is a product pitch — both are ordering strategies taught widely in nonprofit and government financial education.

See [CFPB](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/) debt resources and [FDIC Money Smart](https://www.fdic.gov/consumer-resource-center/money-smart-adults).
