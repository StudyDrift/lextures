# A short honest history and the AI effect

AI research is decades old. Early systems used hand-written rules. Later, statistical machine learning and then deep learning scaled with more data and compute. Generative models for text and images became widely available to the public in the 2020s.

## The "AI effect"

When a technique becomes reliable and familiar, people often stop calling it "AI" and treat it as ordinary software — spell-check, recommendations, fraud alerts. That pattern is sometimes called the **AI effect**: yesterday's AI becomes today's infrastructure.

## What this course will and will not do

We will build a working mental model: definitions, how learning from data works, how generative tools produce outputs, and how to use them critically. We will **not** derive backprop math, train models from scratch, or endorse a single vendor product.

For orientation and further reading, start with [Elements of AI](https://www.elementsofai.com/).
