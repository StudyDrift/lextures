# Compounding Exercise

**Goal:** Compare early-start vs. late-start compounding using the official calculator (learning outcome 5).

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

**Privacy warning:** Use **illustrative or learner-chosen figures only**. Do **not** enter real account numbers, balances, Social Security numbers, passwords, or other sensitive personal data into this assignment or into third-party calculators.

## Tool

Use the [Investor.gov Compound Interest Calculator](https://www.investor.gov/financial-tools-calculators/calculators/compound-interest-calculator).

## Scenario (illustrative inputs — adjust slightly if the UI requires it)

Compare two savers with the **same** monthly contribution and rate, different start ages/years:

| Saver | Years of contributions (illustrative) | Monthly contribution | Annual rate (illustrative) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early start | 40 years | $100 | 5% |
| Late start | 20 years | $100 | 5% |

(If fields differ, keep the comparison fair: same contribution and rate; different time length.)

## Submit

1. **Early-start ending amount** (from calculator).
2. **Late-start ending amount**.
3. **Difference** (early minus late).
4. **One takeaway** (2–4 sentences) about time and compounding — without recommending specific investments.
