# SMART financial goals

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

Vague goals ("save more") are hard to act on. **SMART** goals are:

| Letter | Meaning | Money example |
|---|---|---|
| **S** | Specific | Save for a $1,000 emergency buffer |
| **M** | Measurable | Track balance monthly |
| **A** | Achievable | Based on realistic income after essentials |
| **R** | Relevant | Matches your values (security, independence) |
| **T** | Time-bound | Within 10 months |

## Worked example

- Weak: "I want to get better with money."
- Stronger: "I will set aside $100 each month into a savings account so I have $1,000 in 10 months for unexpected expenses."

## Try it

Draft one short-term goal (under 12 months) and one longer-term goal using SMART. Use illustrative dollar amounts — not real account data.

MyMoney.gov's [MyMoney Five tools](https://www.mymoney.gov/mymoney-five-tools) and [FDIC Money Smart for Adults](https://www.fdic.gov/consumer-resource-center/money-smart-adults) both emphasize goal-setting as a foundation for later budgeting and saving lessons.
