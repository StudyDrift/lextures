# Index funds, fees, and retirement accounts

> **Educational disclaimer:** This course is for **education only**. It is **US-focused** and is **not** personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Rules and figures change — verify current details at official sources linked in each lesson. For decisions about your situation, consider a qualified professional.

## Index funds and fees

An **index fund** aims to track a market index. Education materials often highlight **low costs** because fees compound against you over time. Lower fees are a principle to understand — not a product endorsement.

## Tax-advantaged retirement accounts (U.S.)

Many workers encounter accounts such as a **401(k)** (often through an employer) or an **IRA** (individual retirement arrangement). These accounts can have tax advantages and contribution rules that **change**. Do **not** memorize stale limits from a blog — verify current figures at official sources such as [IRS.gov](https://www.irs.gov/) (as of your reading year).

## Long time horizon

Compounding works best with time. Starting earlier with diversified, low-cost approaches is a common theme in [Investor.gov](https://www.investor.gov/) education and [MyMoney.gov](https://www.mymoney.gov/) Save & Invest materials — still not personalized advice.
